Thoughts from this year:

  1. Discipline goes further than talent; habits stick, motivation alone fluctuates.
  2. A perpetual need to manage time itself is detrimental to the process of time management.
  3. It's a virtue to be bold and forward but it takes prudence to manage it for the right context.
  4. You can't really take words back. Their impact is instant and far reaching, rippling across impressions, perceptions, interpersonal dynamics. It's a balancing act of not only right speech but right action and mind. I think the Noble Eightfold Path categories it better, "Right speech, right action, & right livelihood come under the aggregate of virtue. Right effort, right mindfulness, & right concentration come under the aggregate of concentration. Right view & right resolve come under the aggregate of discernment."
  5. From a Quora post: "When we focus on ourselves, we prevent ourselves from experiencing the world outside of our own head. Living in your head is detrimental, too often, the thoughts in your head are less like a cheerleader squad, and more like a team of football players giving you a swirly. The ability to connect with others starts with the ability to disconnect from yourself. To make a positive impression on others, stop thinking about how charming you’re being (or not being) and stop analyzing what people are thinking about you, instead, start showing curiosity towards other people."
